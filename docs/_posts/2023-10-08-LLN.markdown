## Law of large numbers

The law of large numbers is a theorem that states that if we perform an experiment a large number of times and we take the average of the values that we get during the experiment, this average tends to become the expected value of the experiment as we increase the number of times we perform it. This law is fundamental in probability theory and ensures that, over time, random fluctuations in small samples are mitigated, leading to more reliable predictions and a better understanding of the underlying distribution in large datasets
