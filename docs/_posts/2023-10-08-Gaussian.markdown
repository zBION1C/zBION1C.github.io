## Gaussian distribution
The Gaussian distribution is one the most famous distribution of all statistics. Also known as the normal distribution, is a probability distribution that is symmetric and characterized by its bell-shaped curve. The formula that descrive this distribution is
$$ f(x|\mu,\sigma^2)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}$$
where
- x is a random variable
- $$\mu$$ is the mean of the distribution (where it is centered)
- $$\sigma$$ is the standard deviation (how much wide it is)

It has some nice properties that is worth to note:
- *Simmetry* The distribution is symmetric around its mean, and the mean, mode and median are all equal.
- *Empirical rule* In a normal distribution around 68% of the data falls within one standard deviation from the mean, 95% falls within two standard deviations, and 99.7%  falls within three standard deviations

The Gaussian distribution can be derived as the sum of a large number of independent, identically distributed random variables, regardless of the original distribution. Indeed this sum will approximate the Gaussian distribution.
