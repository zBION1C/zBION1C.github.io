## Functional Central limit theorem and the Donsker's invariance principle
The **Functional central limit theorem** or **Donsker's theorem** is an extension of the central limit theorem.

**Donsker's theorem**: Let $$X_1,X_2,X_3,...$$ be a sequence of independent and identically distributed random variables with mean 0 and variance 1. Let $$S_n=\sum{i=1}{n}X_i$$. The stocastic process $$S:=(S_n)_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$$ is known as a random walk.*Donsker's theorem* assert that the *random walk* stochastic process converges to a **standard brownian motion** as *n* tends to $$\infty$$
