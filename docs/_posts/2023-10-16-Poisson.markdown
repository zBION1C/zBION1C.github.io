## The Poisson process
A Poisson process is a stochastic (random) process that models the occurrence of events over time or space. The key characteristic of a Poisson process is that it gives us a good estimate of the number of events that occur in some intervals of time.

Below we list some charateristics of the Poisson proecess:
- *Independence*: The occurrence of events in disjoint intervals is independent. This means that the number of events in one interval does not affect the number of events in another.

- *Stationarity*: The average rate of event occurrences is constant over time or space. This means that the probability of an event occurring in a small time or space interval is proportional to the length of that interval.

- *Homogeneity*: The process is homogeneous, meaning that the probability of multiple events occurring in a small interval is negligible.

The probability of a random variable X that is distributed according to the Poisson distribution is the following
<center>$$ P(X=k)=\frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^k}{k!}$$</center>
Where
- $$ e $$ is the base of the natural logarithm
- $$ \lambda $$ is the average rate of events
- $$ k! $$ is the number of events in th interval
