## Central limit theorem

The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) states that the distribution of the sum (or average) of independent and identically distributed random variables approaches a normal distribution, regardless of the original distribution's shape. As sample size increases, the resulting distribution becomes increasingly bell-shaped and centered around the true population mean. This fundamental statistical principle enables the use of normal distribution-based methods, even for non-normally distributed data, facilitating accurate statistical inferences and hypothesis testing in various fields.
